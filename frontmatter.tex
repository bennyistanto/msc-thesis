\chapter*{SUMMARY}\thispagestyle{empty}
\noindent BENNY ISTANTO. How Far Can Bias Correction Improve Daily
Satellite Precipitation? Supervised by RIZALDI BOER and I PUTU
SANTIKAYASA.

\medskip
Daily satellite precipitation underpins flood early warning, drought
monitoring, and water-budget analysis, but the Integrated Multi-satellitE
Retrievals for Global Precipitation Measurement (GPM) Late Run (IMERG-L)
carries systematic biases over the Indonesian Maritime Continent. This thesis evaluates a four-stage hybrid
bias correction pipeline: Linear Scaling (LS), Empirical Quantile
Mapping (EQM) with a Gamma fit, Generalized Pareto Distribution (GPD)
tail adjustment, and a station-density-aware Convolutional Neural
Network (CNN) refinement, a Deep Learning (DL) architecture, denoted
LSEQM+DL, applied to IMERG-L over Indonesia for $2001$ to $2025$, with
gauge validation against $171$ independent stations of Indonesia's
Meteorological, Climatological, and Geophysical Agency (BMKG).

\medskip
Against the out-of-sample BMKG network, three of the four verification
pillars move close to the gauge target. Relative bias drops from
$-11.4\%$ for the raw Linear-Scaling stage to $-0.6\%$ for LSEQM+DL, the
standard-deviation ratio moves from $0.71$ to $1.00$, the
Kolmogorov-Smirnov $p$-value rises from $0.01\%$ to $19\%$, and the
ratios at the $95$th and $99$th percentiles move from $0.74$ and $0.71$
to $1.05$ and $1.01$. Event detection shows a designed trade-off: the
corrected product detects fewer light-rain events than the raw retrieval
below $10$~mm/day but achieves higher critical success at every threshold
above $20$~mm/day, with a $57\%$ Critical Success Index (CSI) gain at the $50$~mm/day
threshold ($0.097$ versus $0.062$), the regime in which BMKG flood
early-warning advisories operate.

\medskip
One diagnostic does not move. The Pearson correlation between paired
daily satellite and gauge values stays near $0.34$ across all four stages
and stays in the narrow band $[0.332, 0.348]$ across all fifteen settings
of a Bali sensitivity sweep over the blending weight, the GPD threshold
percentile, and the station-density saturation count. This is the
predicted behaviour of marginal bias correction, where rank preservation
and variance inflation without skill bound the attainable correlation at
the timing quality of the raw retrieval. A post-hoc diagnostic using the half-hourly IMERG-L source shows that
approximately two-thirds of the apparent ceiling is attributable to a
calendar-window mismatch between the Coordinated Universal Time (UTC)
daily IMERG aggregate and the morning-observation BMKG gauge convention
specified by Head of BMKG Decree SK.32/TL.202/KB/BMG-2006: the
window-aligned upper bound is approximately $r \approx 0.62$ rather
than $r \approx 0.34$. Re-aggregating the daily IMERG-L archive to a
morning-to-morning window matched to the BMKG convention would
therefore lift the headline Pearson $r$ from approximately $0.34$ to
approximately $0.62$ without changing any other pipeline output, and is
identified as the most operationally actionable next step in the
discussion. The corrected product
is therefore suitable for applications that depend on the daily
distribution and not for applications that depend on day-specific
timing.

\medskip
The pipeline is released as an open, configuration-driven
implementation whose reproducibility is demonstrated by re-executing
the headline results end-to-end on free cloud infrastructure: the Bali
subdomain ($9 \times 14$ grid at $0.1^{\circ}$, $80$ land pixels, all
$36$ dekadal windows of the $2001$ to $2025$ record) completes in
$72.1$ minutes on a standard Google Colab Central Processing Unit (CPU)
runtime.

\medskip
\noindent Keywords: daily satellite precipitation, gauge validation,
hybrid bias correction, IMERG, reproducibility

\chapter*{RINGKASAN}\thispagestyle{empty}
\noindent BENNY ISTANTO. Sejauh Mana Koreksi Bias Mampu Memperbaiki Data
Presipitasi Satelit Harian? Dibimbing oleh RIZALDI BOER dan I PUTU
SANTIKAYASA.

\medskip
Produk satelit presipitasi harian menopang peringatan dini banjir,
pemantauan kekeringan, dan analisis neraca air; namun, \emph{Integrated
Multi-satellitE Retrievals for Global Precipitation Measurement} (GPM)
\emph{Late Run} (IMERG-L) mengandung bias sistematis di Benua Maritim
Indonesia. Penelitian ini mengevaluasi \emph{pipeline} koreksi bias
hibrida empat tahap: \emph{Linear Scaling} (LS), \emph{Empirical
Quantile Mapping} (EQM) berbasis sebaran Gamma, penyesuaian ekor
dengan \emph{Generalized Pareto Distribution} (GPD), dan penyempurnaan
berbasis \emph{Convolutional Neural Network} (CNN) yang
mempertimbangkan kerapatan stasiun, sebuah arsitektur \emph{Deep
Learning} (DL), disingkat LSEQM+DL. \emph{Pipeline} diterapkan pada
IMERG-L di Indonesia untuk periode $2001$ hingga $2025$, dengan
validasi terhadap $171$ stasiun Badan Meteorologi, Klimatologi, dan
Geofisika (BMKG) yang tidak digunakan dalam koreksi.

\medskip
Terhadap jaringan BMKG yang dipisahkan untuk validasi, tiga dari empat
pilar verifikasi bergerak mendekati target pengamatan stasiun: bias
relatif dari $-11{,}4\%$ (LS) menjadi $-0{,}6\%$ (LSEQM+DL), rasio
simpangan baku dari $0{,}71$ menuju $1{,}00$, nilai-$p$
Kolmogorov-Smirnov dari $0{,}01\%$ menjadi $19\%$, serta rasio
persentil ke-$95$ dan ke-$99$ dari $0{,}74$ dan $0{,}71$ menuju
$1{,}05$ dan $1{,}01$. Pendeteksian kejadian menunjukkan
\emph{trade-off} yang dirancang: produk terkoreksi mendeteksi lebih
sedikit kejadian hujan ringan di bawah $10$~mm/hari, namun mencapai
keberhasilan kritis yang lebih tinggi pada setiap ambang di atas
$20$~mm/hari, dengan peningkatan \emph{Critical Success Index} (CSI)
sebesar $57\%$ pada ambang $50$~mm/hari ($0{,}097$ dibanding
$0{,}062$), yaitu rentang ambang yang relevan dengan peringatan dini
banjir BMKG.

\medskip
Satu diagnostik tidak berubah: korelasi Pearson antara nilai harian
satelit dan stasiun yang dipasangkan tetap pada $0{,}34$ di seluruh
empat tahap dan dalam pita $[0{,}332;~0{,}348]$ di seluruh lima belas
pengaturan \emph{sweep} sensitivitas Bali (bobot pencampuran, ambang
GPD, saturasi kerapatan stasiun). Perilaku ini merupakan prediksi
teoretis dari koreksi bias berbasis sebaran marginal: pemeliharaan
peringkat dan inflasi ragam tanpa keterampilan temporal membatasi
korelasi pada kualitas penentuan waktu \emph{retrieval} satelit
mentah. Diagnostik \emph{post-hoc} menggunakan sumber IMERG-L
setengah-jaman menunjukkan bahwa sekitar dua per tiga dari batas
yang teramati disebabkan oleh ketidaksesuaian jendela kalender
antara agregasi harian IMERG dalam waktu UTC dan konvensi
pengamatan pagi stasiun BMKG yang diatur oleh Keputusan Kepala
BMG Nomor SK.32/TL.202/KB/BMG-2006: batas atas pada jendela yang
selaras adalah sekitar $r \approx 0{,}62$, bukan $r \approx 0{,}34$.
Pengagregasian ulang arsip harian IMERG-L pada jendela
pagi-ke-pagi yang selaras dengan konvensi BMKG karenanya akan
mengangkat Pearson $r$ utama dari sekitar $0{,}34$ ke sekitar
$0{,}62$ tanpa mengubah keluaran \emph{pipeline} lainnya, dan
ditetapkan sebagai langkah lanjutan yang paling dapat
dioperasionalkan dalam pembahasan. Produk terkoreksi
karenanya sesuai untuk aplikasi yang bergantung pada sifat
sebaran, tetapi kurang sesuai untuk aplikasi yang bergantung pada
ketepatan waktu harian.

\medskip
\emph{Pipeline} dirilis sebagai implementasi terbuka berbasis berkas
konfigurasi; reproduktibilitasnya ditunjukkan dengan mengeksekusi
ulang hasil utama secara menyeluruh pada infrastruktur awan gratis:
\emph{subdomain} Bali ($9 \times 14$ piksel pada $0{,}1^{\circ}$, $80$
piksel daratan, $36$ jendela dekadik $2001$-$2025$) selesai dalam
$72{,}1$ menit pada \emph{Central Processing Unit} (CPU) standar
Google Colab.

\medskip
\noindent Kata kunci: presipitasi satelit harian, validasi stasiun,
koreksi bias hibrida, IMERG, reproduktibilitas

\chapter*{ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS}\thispagestyle{empty}
\textit{Alhamdulillah}, this thesis has been completed.

This thesis grew out of a simple question: how far can bias correction
actually improve a daily satellite precipitation field? Answering it became a
study of what statistical correction can and cannot do, together with a
small open-source pipeline that anyone can re-run. I hope both the finding
and the code prove useful to others working on satellite precipitation,
